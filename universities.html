UNIVERSITIES AND CUT OFF MARKS 🎓

Choosing the right university starts with understanding cut-off marks. These are the minimum scores required in Joint Admissions and Matriculation Board (JAMB) to qualify for admission into a course or institution.

Cut-off marks vary by:

  • University
  • Course of study
  • Competition level that year

🏫 What is a Cut-Off Mark?

A cut-off mark is the minimum score a candidate must achieve in JAMB to be eligible for admission into a specific course at a university. It serves as a benchmark for universities to select candidates based on their performance in the JAMB examination.

📊 How Are Cut-Off Marks Determined?

Cut-off marks are determined by universities based on several factors, including:

  • Number of applicants for a course
  • Performance of candidates in JAMB
  • Available slots for the course
  • Previous years' cut-off trends

📈 Importance of Cut-Off Marks

Cut-off marks are crucial for students as they determine eligibility for admission into desired courses and universities. Meeting or exceeding the cut-off mark increases the chances of securing a place in the preferred institution.

🔍 Checking Cut-Off Marks

Students can check cut-off marks for various universities and courses on the official websites of the institutions or through the JAMB portal.

Types of Cut-Off Marks

There are different types of cut-off marks, including:

  • University Cut-Off Mark: The minimum score required for admission to a specific university which usually determined by the university itself.
  • Departmental Cut-Off Mark: The minimum score required for admission into a specific course, which can vary based on the popularity and competitiveness of the course.

How is aggregate or cut off mark calculated in unilag?

The cut-off mark for UNILAG is calculated based on the candidate's performance in the JAMB examination, WASSCE, and POST-UTME examinations.

The formula for calculating the aggregate score is as follows:

  1. JAMB: 50
  2. WASSCE: 20
  3. POST-UTME: 30

Total: 100

For JAMB: since jamb is over 400 how does unilag derive 50? It’s simple so your jamb score is divided by 8 E.g let say a candidate scores 350 out 400 in JAMB thes score is divided by 8 i.e 350/8 which is equal to 43.75

For WASSCE: WAEC results are converted to a 20-point grading system for admission purposes.

Each grade is assigned a specific point value:

  • A1 = 4.0
  • B2 = 3.6
  • B3 = 3.2
  • C4 = 2.8
  • C6 = 2.0

To qualify for admission, a student must have at least five (5) credit passes (minimum of C6) in core subjects relevant to their chosen course.

Example:

Suppose a student has the following grades:

  • English – C4 = 2.8
  • Mathematics – A1 = 4.0
  • Further Mathematics – B2 = 3.6
  • Chemistry – B3 = 3.2
  • Physics – B2 = 3.6

Total: 2.8 + 4.0 + 3.6 + 3.2 + 3.6 = 17.2

Therefore, the student’s WAEC aggregate score is 17.2 out of 20 (17.2/20).

For POST-UTME:In UNILAG, the Post-UTME exam is scored over 40 questions, but the final score is converted to 30 marks for admission calculations.

To convert the Post-UTME score to the 30-point scale,Your raw score (out of 40) is divided by 40 Then multiplied by 30

Example: Suppose a student scores 35 out of 40:

35 ÷ 40 = 0.875

0.875 × 30 = 26.25

Therefore, the student’s Post-UTME score is 26.25 out of 30.

To calculate the total aggregate score for UNILAG, you would add the JAMB score (converted to 50), the WAEC score (converted to 20), and the Post-UTME score (converted to 30).

Using the example above:

  • JAMB: 43.75
  • WAEC: 17.2
  • POST-UTME: 26.25

Total: 43.75 + 17.2 + 26.25 = 87.2

In this example, the student’s aggregate score for UNILAG would be 87.2 out of 100. This score is very high and cangain admission into courses like medcine and Computer Science which are the most competitive courses in UNILAG
